🧠 How Professional Traders Read Options Data

PCR as Contrarian Sentiment

When PCR <0.70, there are far more calls open than puts — the market is crowded bullish but protected. Institutional hedging makes sharp falls less likely. This is when you buy calls cheaply, before the crowd notices. When PCR >1.30, the reverse is true — buy puts before the pullback comes.

IV Rank: Buy When Cheap, Exit When Expensive

Professional options traders never buy options when IV is high — you're overpaying for every rupee of premium. IV Rank <30% means options are cheap relative to their own history. When volatility mean-reverts upward (which it always does), you profit from both the price move AND the volatility expansion (vega profit). This is a double tailwind.

OI Buildup = Follow the Big Money

Volume shows who traded today. OI shows who is still committed. A 15–30% spike in OI at a specific strike means institutional money just opened a large position there. These strikes tend to act as magnets or walls. Fresh OI buildup in calls = smart money betting on upside = time to consider calls.

Max Pain: The Expiry Magnet

Market makers are short options across all strikes. They're financially incentivized to pin the index near the level where the most options expire worthless — the Max Pain strike. In the last trading session of expiry week, price gravitates toward Max Pain roughly 70% of the time. Use this to bias your direction near expiry.

Delta: The Strike Sweet Spot

Δ 0.30–0.50 options give the best risk-reward for directional trades. Δ <0.20 = lottery ticket (needs a massive move). Δ >0.65 = expensive, behaves like the underlying itself. The 0.25–0.60 zone gives you enough leverage to make money on a moderate move without paying too much premium.

Gamma: Responsiveness

Gamma tells you how fast your delta changes as the market moves. High gamma (near ATM) = your option responds sharply to every tick. Low gamma (far OTM or ITM) = slow response. For momentum trades, favor higher gamma strikes. For positional swings, lower gamma gives more stable delta exposure.
